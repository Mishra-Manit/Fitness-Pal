1. Upper Body Too Low

This happens because your upper body is too close to your knees. If you are squatting with a lot of weight, this will kill your back. Make sure your upper body is always upright. This will save you back pain and will stop you from getting injured. Try this the next time you squat, and see how much stress will be relieved from your back.


2. Not Going Deep Enough

This happens when you don't go all the way down on your squats. Going all the way is very beneficial while squatting. It uses more muscle fibers and engages the lower back. Going to your natural squat depth is very important and will benefit you in the long run. Make sure you squat all the way down the next time you squat.


3. Ankle Bent At Too High Of An Angle

This occurs when your ankles are bent at a very high angle. This is a very common error which results in injuries. Bent ankles can result in things such as twisted ankles. This is a very easy error to fix, and will provide your squat with a lot more support.
