Example Of A Good Curl


Your Scores Are Ready!


1. Stand Straight

This happens because your body is not straight during your curl. This would cause the excercise to have less of an effect on your bicep because of the momentum caused by leaning. To avoid leaning try keeping your back straight when doing the curls, if it helps lean back a little bit while doing your curl


2. Low Range of Motion

This happens when your arms arn't fully extended during the curl. A low range of motion hinders gains and doesn't allow you to maximize your time. Going through the full range of motion allows you to target all areas of the bicep and will allow you to get stronger faster. To avoid low range of motion extend your arm fully when going down on the curl.


3. Bent Knees

This occurs when your knees are bent during curls. To avoid this error showing up, make sure your knees are not bent when you are doing curls. Bending your knees will make you use momentum to lift the weight instead of using your biceps. Make sure your knees are straight.
